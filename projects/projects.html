SnapShell – AI Email Assistant

September 2025

SnapShell is an AI-powered Chrome extension built during ShellHacks 2025 for the Google Cloud and Assurant challenge. It assists users by sorting, summarizing, and drafting email responses directly within their inbox. Developed using Google’s ADK, Gemini 2.5 Flash, Python, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, the extension integrates on the client side for secure and private AI assistance. I led a team of developers to create a seamless interface, optimize API calls, and present our final product to judges. SnapShell showcases my strength in AI integration, teamwork, and full-stack innovation under time constraints.

RenArc Remodeling Website

July 2025 – Present

RenArc is a fully functional business website developed for a remodeling and renovation company. Designed and launched from the ground up, it emphasizes performance, usability, and clean UI/UX design. Built using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap, Tailwind CSS, and Flask, it supports dual form submissions — one for clients and another for admin operations. The backend streamlines scheduling, while the responsive front end adapts beautifully across devices. RenArc showcases my full-stack development skills and ability to deliver production-quality systems to real clients.

GuiltBytes

September 2024

GuiltBytes was my first-ever hackathon project, built during ShellHacks 2024. It’s a web app that helps users track unhealthy eating habits, calculate BMI, and view personalized health statistics. Created using HTML, CSS, JavaScript, and Bootstrap, GuiltBytes introduced me to responsive design and data-driven user interfaces. The experience taught me how to take an idea from concept to completion in under 36 hours — sparking my passion for practical, user-oriented software development.
